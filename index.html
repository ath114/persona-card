Edward The "Floating Funds" Roommate

Student House Organizer

Lives with 4 other students who owe him money. Uses group chats and Venmo, but often gets “left on read.” “I currently have about $80 floating out there that I haven't been paid back for, and I have to remind my roommates three times just to get a Venmo response.”

Goals

  • Financial Fairness: Wants a system to accurately split bills that contain both shared household goods and personal items.

  • Operational Efficiency: Prefers a standard shopping schedule so everyone knows what to expect and avoids "fridge tetris" caused by buying duplicate items like milk or eggs.

  • Timely Communication: Desires a convenient way to update the household on what food is needed before someone goes to the store.

Frustrations

  • Administrative Friction: Exhausted by the "manual work" and "human initiative" required to calculate totals and request payments. "Usually, I notice we are out of trash bags or olive oil. I text our group chat, 'Hey, we need X.' Everyone leaves me on read."

  • Social Stress: Feels awkwardness and stress when roommates are unwilling to cooperate or pay back their share promptly. "I calculate the total, divide it by four, and send Venmo requests. Then I have to remind them three times to actually pay me."

  • Inconsistent Contributions: Frustrated by the "rotation system" where one person buys luxury items and the next buys the cheapest possible version. "The 'rotation' system fails when someone buys the cheapest, single-roll toilet paper when it is their turn, after I bought the luxury 24-pack."
